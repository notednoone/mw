About Me

My background, philosophy, and what drives me

My fascination with technology began in the 80's with a Commodore 64 and later an IBM 386, alongside my father's Vectrex. While initially captivated by the games, my interest deepened after moving to California in 1995 at 18 to work at Sun Microsystems. It was there, where my father was a mechanical engineer, that he told me they were hiring for Desktop Manufacturing.

At the time, there was a buzz at Sun about the upcoming "JAVA" product - the buildings had big purple signs eveywhere with the name. I eventually moved to Server Manufacturing with a growing desire to understand and work with that technology.

After advancing to technical leadership at Sun, I transitioned to Siebel Systems for a couple of years. Later, I took a life-changing leap into freelance technical consulting, developing relationships with companies like Intel, AMD, and other tech leaders.

Today, with over three decades in the industry, I've been fortunate to work in diverse roles from manufacturing to consulting to executive management, acquiring a deep understanding of how technology works, how to effectively manage teams, and how to build companies that drive innovation. I now run LIFTOFF Digital, bringing long-standing relationships in the industry together with the latest in automation and AI technologies.
